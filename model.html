bmlm: Model details

Matti Vuorre

2016-07-14

The specification for the multilevel mediation model is as follows:

\begin{equation} \begin{aligned} Y_{i} &\sim Normal(\mu_{Yi}, \sigma_Y) \\ \mu_{Yi} &= (D_Y + d_{Y[id]}) + (C' + c'_{[id]}) X_{i} + (B + b_{[id]}) M_{i} \\ M_{ij} &\sim Normal(\mu_{Mi}, \sigma_M) \\ \mu_{Mi} &= (D_M + d_{M[id]}) + (A + a_{[id]}) X_{ij} \\ \begin{pmatrix} {c'_{[id]}}\\{b_{[id]}}\\{a_{[id]}}\\{d_{Y[id]}}\\{d_{M[id]}} \end{pmatrix} &\sim MVNorm \begin{pmatrix} 0, \Sigma\end {pmatrix} \end{aligned} \end{equation}

Where \(\Sigma\) is a covariance matrix of the participant-level varying effects, constructed from a correlation matrix \(\Omega\) and vector of standard deviations \(\tau\). These operations are optimized by Cholesky factorization to speed up the MCMC sampling procedures (Stan Development Team, 2016, sec. 6.12).

The priors are defined as follows (text annotation indicates that users can set these by giving the named argument to mlm()):

\begin{equation} \begin{aligned} D_Y, D_M &\sim Normal(0, intrcpt\_scale) \\ A, B, C' &\sim Normal(0, prior\_scale) \\ \tau &\sim Cauchy^+(0, tau\_scale) \\ \Omega &\sim LKJcorr(2) \end{aligned} \end{equation}

The average-level total effect of X on Y is given by (Kenny, Korchmaros, & Bolger, 2003):

\begin{equation} \begin{aligned} C &= C' + AB + \Sigma_{a_{j}b_{j}} \end{aligned} \end{equation}
